Cookie Policy

Learn how we use session cookies, local storage variables, and responsive caches to provide a seamless dark/light farmer experience.

Last Updated: May 23, 2026
1. What are Cookies and Local Storage?

Cookies are small text files stored by your web browser on your computer or mobile device. Local Storage (localStorage) is a similar built-in browser memory vault that allows websites to remember configurations and preferences locally without slowing down page transitions.

2. Essential Keys and Cookies We Use

BananaGuard AI operates with the absolute minimum set of technical tracking elements required to provide functional value. We do not run intrusive commercial cross-site tracking beacons.

Key/Cookie Name Type Purpose & Lifetime
sessionid Secure Session Stores unique token identifying your logged-in farmer account. Keeps you signed in as you navigate between Scanner, Dashboard, and History. Expires when you log out.
csrftoken Security Protects our web application form actions (like registration, logins, and image uploads) from Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF) exploits. Essential security utility.
leafai-theme Local Storage Remembers your design theme preference (light or dark mode). Prevents page flicker when loading custom colors on high-contrast AMOLED mobile screens. Indefinite lifetime unless cleared.
leafai-lang Local Storage Stores your selected UI language (English, Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, or Kannada) so the interface loads in your preferred language automatically.
3. Third-Party CDNs

Our application structure relies on trusted, high-performance Content Delivery Networks (CDNs):

  • Bootstrap CSS CDN: Speeds up design layout rendering.
  • Bootstrap Icons CDN: Delivers clean visual icons for navigation and statuses.
  • Google Fonts CDN: Loads modern visual typography (Plus Jakarta Sans & Inter).

These CDNs do not place commercial tracking cookies on your device but may record standard connection headers (IP address, user agent) to compile latency stats.

4. Managing Your Preferences

You can block or delete cookies by adjusting your browser security preferences. However, please note that disabling the essential session or CSRF cookies will prevent you from logging in, accessing the crop dashboard, or running leaf diagnostics.
