IMPORTANT WARNING FOR FARMERS

The disease diagnostic outcomes, probability indexes, and recommended solutions generated by BananaGuard AI are derived from a deep convolutional neural network (MobileNetV2). Artificial intelligence models are statistical approximation engines. They are subject to false positives, false negatives, and misclassifications caused by lighting variations, camera resolutions, soil dust, or leaf age. Never execute wholesale crop chemical sprays or field eradications solely based on this application.

1. AI Recommendation Limits

Our neural network model classifies banana leaf conditions into specific categories (including Healthy, Black Sigatoka, Yellow Sigatoka, Moko, Panama, Cordana, Pestalotiopsis, Insect Pest Damage, and Bract Mosaic Virus) based on spatial patterns in leaf cells.

  • Statistical Classifier: A high confidence percentage (e.g., 95% Black Sigatoka) means the visual patterns in the image match our trained datasets. It does not replace field biology tests.
  • Image Limitations: Obscured leaf angles, shadows, moisture, or insect bites might confuse the neural network and result in inaccurate recommendations.
  • Dynamic Environments: Weather patterns, crop genetics, and local soil conditions have immense influence on plant pathology. General AI recommendations cannot capture local nuances.
2. Field-Testing Protocols

Before applying any systemic fungicides, chemical bactericides, or biological control remedies suggested by our platform, we strongly require farmers to implement the following safeguards:

Small Patch Trials

Test any suggested spray, organic compound, or neem oil preparation on a single, isolated plant leaf or small patch. Monitor the patch for 48 hours for signs of phytotoxicity or chemical burn before wider application.

Expert Consultations

Always share diagnostic photos and findings with your local government agricultural extension officer, a certified agronomist, or district plant pathologist before taking destructive actions (like rogueing or quarantines).

3. Notifiable Biosecurity Threats

Specific banana leaf infections—most notably Panama Disease (Fusarium Wilt TR4) and Moko Bacterial Wilt—are categorized as highly infectious, devastating agricultural quarantine pests.

If BananaGuard AI flags your leaf with high probability of Panama or Moko disease, you MUST report this immediately to your district quarantine officers and agronomists. Incorrect self-treatment can lead to catastrophic regional crop failures and biosecurity violations.
